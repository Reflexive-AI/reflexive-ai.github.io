## Translation Principles

1. **Institutional terminology**: Where the EU AI Act or other regulatory instruments have established official translations in Spanish or Catalan, those translations take precedence.
2. **English retention**: Terms such as "red teaming," "guardrails," "jailbreaking," "sandboxing," "fine-tuning," and "RLHF" are retained in English because they are widely used in their original form in both Spanish and Catalan technical discourse. A parenthetical gloss provides the nearest equivalent.
3. **Gender-neutral language**: Definitions avoid gendered constructions where possible, consistent with current academic norms in both languages.
4. **Catalan specificity**: Catalan translations follow Institut d'Estudis Catalans (IEC) orthographic norms, including the use of the interpunct (punt volat) in "l·l" and other standard conventions.
5. **Latin American compatibility**: Spanish translations use standard vocabulary that is acceptable across both Latin American and peninsular usage (e.g., "computacional" rather than region-specific alternatives).
